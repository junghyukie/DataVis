Key Insights from Migration Data

Major Findings:

  • Peak Migration Year: 2023 showed the highest migration numbers, reflecting post-pandemic recovery and economic growth in New Zealand
  • Top Source Countries: India, China, and Philippines consistently rank in top 3, representing over 40% of total migration
  • Age Demographics: The 20-29 age group represents the largest migrant population (1.67M people, 36% of total), followed by 30-39 years (1.17M, 25%)
  • Primary Purpose: Work visa holders constitute the majority of permanent migrants, indicating strong labor market demand
  • Geographic Trends: Asian countries show increasing migration trends since 2015, while European migration remains stable
  • Seasonal Patterns: Migration peaks typically occur during Q1 and Q4, aligning with academic and fiscal year cycles

Strategic Recommendations:

  • Targeted Recruitment: Focus immigration campaigns on 20-39 age demographics with skilled work backgrounds
  • Infrastructure Investment: Strengthen visa processing capacity for high-volume countries (India, China, Philippines)
  • Integration Programs: Develop specialized settlement services for work visa holders to improve retention rates
  • Policy Adaptation: Monitor post-pandemic migration pattern shifts and adjust immigration quotas accordingly
  • Regional Distribution: Incentivize migration to regional areas to balance population growth across New Zealand
  • Student Pipeline: Enhance student visa to work visa pathways to retain educated talent

Data Quality Notes:

  • Data source: New Zealand Department of Statistics (2013-2023)
  • Dataset includes both permanent and long-term migration (12+ months)
  • Excludes short-term visitors and tourists (under 12 months)
  • Country classifications follow ISO 3166-1 standards
